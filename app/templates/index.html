About Semaphore

Semaphore, an ancient form of visual communication, uses flags, lights, or arm movements to transmit information over distances. Historically, semaphore systems date back to the late 18th century when the French engineer Claude Chappe introduced the first practical semaphore line, a network of towers with pivoting arms that could encode messages. This innovation significantly improved communication speed compared to couriers or messengers, playing a crucial role in military and governmental operations.

In modern times, semaphore remains a symbolic and functional communication method in specialized areas such as maritime signaling and scouting. It exemplifies ingenuity in non-verbal communication and serves as a reminder of the evolution of human connectivity.
